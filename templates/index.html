Hello

I'm Erik. In 2010, I found a MIT course on iTunesU which taught me how to build algorithms. Although creating most algorithms was difficult initially, and challenges continue to present themselves, I find the practice of using computers to solve problems fascinating. This site contains projects that fully engaged my mind in the most enjoyable way. I'm publishing these works hoping that other people who might be interested in computer science can learn something new and see a side of computation not always available. Many people see computers as a black box that does magic and if everything goes well, you get the desired results. With these projects I'm aiming to make that black box a slightly lighter shade of grey which might help others understand how computers can be instructed to solve hard problems quickly.

Learning is usually easiest when the student already has an understanding of the context of the challenge. Here you have a few contexts from which to choose. Like chess? Check out the Knight's Tour. More of a Scrabble® fan? Use my algorithm Anagreat to see how a computer can take a sequence of letters and create every possible combination of those letters in under a second

This site is currently under construction. It's not pretty, but it does cool stuff!
